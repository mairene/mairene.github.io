In short, I am a huge believer of "if not A, then B"​ when it comes to the rat-race of career pursuit; if choice A is not working, then go after choice B. I've always fervently gone down paths I've been passionate about, which has led me from being a risk analyst at Citigroup to being a medical assistant in Papua New Guinea in pursuit of the health industry with hopes of attacking "big world problems"​. I realized the best way to contribute was to combine both my love for building something meaningful and impactful on the world with my technically-prone mind: I am a newly-minted full stack engineer. I spent two years in Excel manipulating and analyzing data in the financial services industry right out of college. I found that the theoretical logic and proofs I learned in school were not being used and my propensity for number understanding was not being challenged. Most importantly, I realized that my technical skills did not amount to much if they were not being applied in an industry I cared for. I switched to dabbling my way through various roles in the most altruistic industry I could think of at the time: the medical industry. It was not until my most recent role as a content intern at a health non-profit did the need to fulfill the technical side of me become apparent. I have the experience in a data setting, the big-picture hope of helping people, and the aesthetic-eye from the part-time job I worked on the side in a design-focused Japanese company (MUJI). Having now dipped my foot in some aspects of back-end and front-end, I am eager to be on the developer side and simultaneously keep design in mind in an industry that matters to me. I am now a software developer with a propensity for logic games and a conscience stacked with altruistic dreams. I hope to find a blend of these two facets in a career and aspire to be an active contributor and constant learner in the developer community.
